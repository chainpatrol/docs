## Reporting Threats

### Can customers report threats directly to us?

Yes! Customers can report threats through multiple channels:

<Tabs>
  <Tab title="Telegram" icon="telegram">
    **Direct Group Reporting**
    
    Share threats directly in your dedicated Telegram group for immediate visibility.
    
    **Best for:**
    - Quick threat reports
    - Screenshots and URLs
    - Real-time discussions
    - Urgent threats requiring immediate attention
  </Tab>
  
  <Tab title="Slack" icon="slack">
    **Slack Connect Channel**
    
    Post threats in your Slack Connect channel for team collaboration.
    
    **Best for:**
    - Enterprise workflows
    - Team coordination
    - Documented threat history
    - Integration with internal tools
  </Tab>
  
  <Tab title="Bot Integrations" icon="robot">
    **Automated Bot Reporting**
    
    Use our automated bot integrations to streamline reporting without leaving your workflow.
    
    **Benefits:**
    - Automated threat submission
    - Consistent formatting
    - Faster processing
    - Reduced manual work
    
    <Tip>
    Ask your point of contact for setup instructions to enable bot integrations.
    </Tip>
  </Tab>
</Tabs>

## Major Events

If your organization has upcoming launches, announcements, or major events, communication is critical:

<Steps>
  <Step title="Share Event Details in Advance">
    Notify us as soon as you have event details to share
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="We Prepare Resources">
    This allows us to dedicate extra monitoring resources and prepare response plans
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Enhanced Protection">
    We can detect and take action on threats faster during your critical moments
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Warning>
**Why this matters:** Phishing attacks are often timed to coincide with your activities. Product launches, token sales, airdrops, and major announcements are prime targets for scammers.
</Warning>

### What to Share

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Event Type & Date" icon="calendar">
    - Product launches
    - Token sales or airdrops
    - Partnership announcements
    - Rebrands or major updates
    - Conference appearances
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="Expected Impact" icon="chart-line">
    - Anticipated traffic volume
    - Social media campaign details
    - Press coverage expectations
    - Community engagement plans
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="New Assets" icon="link">
    - Landing pages or microsites
    - New social media accounts
    - Campaign-specific domains
    - Promotional materials
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="Timing & Duration" icon="clock">
    - Event start and end dates
    - Peak activity windows
    - Geographic focus areas
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

<Check>
**Better prepared, better protected:** The earlier we know, the faster we can detect and take action on threats.
</Check>

## Organization Assets

### Keeping Your Asset List Current

We need an up-to-date list of your organization's assets to protect them effectively.

<CardGroup cols={3}>
  <Card title="Regular Updates" icon="arrows-rotate">
    Review and update your asset list periodically (at least quarterly)
  </Card>
  
  <Card title="New Assets" icon="plus">
    Notify us immediately when you launch new domains, social accounts, or other assets
  </Card>
  
  <Card title="How to Update" icon="gear">
    Contact your point of contact or use the ChainPatrol dashboard
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

<Info>
The more complete your asset list, the better we can protect your brand and avoid false positives.
</Info>

### When to Update Your Assets

<Tabs>
  <Tab title="Immediate Updates" icon="bolt">
    **Notify us immediately for:**
    - New domain registrations
    - New social media accounts
    - New mobile applications
    - New blockchain addresses
    - New team member accounts
    - Acquired brands or companies
  </Tab>
  
  <Tab title="Regular Reviews" icon="calendar-check">
    **Schedule quarterly reviews for:**
    - Deprecated assets to remove
    - Temporary campaign assets
    - Employee turnover updates
    - Rebranded assets
    - Changed contact information
  </Tab>
  
  <Tab title="Event-Driven Updates" icon="calendar-days">
    **Update before major events:**
    - Product launches
    - Marketing campaigns
    - Partnership announcements
    - Organizational changes
  </Tab>
</Tabs>

## Threat Snapshots

### What are threat snapshots?

Threat snapshots are point-in-time records of detected threats targeting your organization.

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="Complete View" icon="camera">
    A comprehensive record of the threat at the moment of detection
  </Card>
  
  <Card title="Visual Evidence" icon="image">
    Screenshots and archived content
  </Card>
  
  <Card title="Technical Details" icon="server">
    Domains, IPs, and hosting information
  </Card>
  
  <Card title="Action Timeline" icon="timeline">
    When detected and when action was taken
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

### What data do they contain?

Each snapshot includes comprehensive information:

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Visual Evidence" icon="image">
    - Screenshots of the malicious content
    - Archived copies of web pages
    - Social media post captures
    - Visual similarity analysis
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="Technical Metadata" icon="code">
    - Domain information (WHOIS, DNS records)
    - Hosting details (provider, IP addresses)
    - Infrastructure data (CDN, nameservers)
    - SSL certificate information
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="Detection Details" icon="magnifying-glass">
    - When the threat was first detected
    - Which detection rules triggered
    - Confidence scores and risk levels
    - AI analysis results
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="Action History" icon="list-check">
    - Takedown requests submitted
    - Provider responses received
    - Status updates and timeline
    - Final resolution outcome
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

<Tip>
Threat snapshots are available in your ChainPatrol dashboard and can be exported for internal reporting or compliance purposes.
</Tip>
