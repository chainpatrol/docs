## What Are Legal Documents?

**Legal documents** are official documents that prove a company's ownership rights, authorization, or identity when requesting the removal of harmful or fraudulent content.

<Info>
Many online platforms require verified legal documentation before they will act on a takedown request, especially when dealing with impersonation, trademark misuse, or brand-related abuse.
</Info>

### Why They're Required

These documents help providers confirm that the takedown request is legitimate and submitted by an authorized party.

## Types of Legal Documents

ChainPatrol typically works with the following categories of legal documentation:

<Tabs>
  <Tab title="Trademark Registration" icon="trademark">
    **Proof of brand ownership**
    
    <Card title="What It Is" icon="file-certificate">
      Official documentation proving that an organization legally owns a brand name, logo, or trademark.
    </Card>
    
    **When It's Required:**
    - Content is impersonating a brand
    - Misusing protected intellectual property
    - Claiming false association with trademark
    - Using brand elements without authorization
    
    **What It Includes:**
    - Trademark registration number
    - Registration date and jurisdiction
    - Description of protected marks
    - Visual representations (logos, wordmarks)
    - Goods and services covered
    
    **Common Formats:**
    - USPTO registration certificates (United States)
    - EUIPO certificates (European Union)
    - National trademark office documents
    - International (Madrid Protocol) registrations
    
    <Note>
    Providers often require this when content is impersonating a brand or misusing protected intellectual property.
    </Note>
  </Tab>
  
  <Tab title="Letter of Authorization (LOA)" icon="file-signature">
    **Authorization to act on behalf**
    
    <Card title="What It Is" icon="file-lines">
      A document in which the brand owner declares that ChainPatrol is authorized to submit takedown requests on their behalf.
    </Card>
    
    **Key Components:**
    - Organization's legal name and details
    - Statement of authorization
    - Specific actions ChainPatrol is authorized to perform
    - Authorized representative's signature
    - Date of authorization
    - Contact information
    
    **Typical Use Cases:**
    - Social media takedowns (especially Twitter/X)
    - App store removal requests
    - Hosting provider complaints
    - General brand protection activities
    
    **Example Statement:**
    > "[Organization Name] hereby authorizes ChainPatrol to submit takedown requests, abuse reports, and content removal requests on our behalf for the purpose of protecting our brand and trademarks from impersonation, fraud, and unauthorized use."
    
    <Tip>
    LOAs are typically simpler and faster to obtain than Power of Attorney documents.
    </Tip>
  </Tab>
  
  <Tab title="Power of Attorney (POA)" icon="gavel">
    **Formal legal authorization**
    
    <Card title="What It Is" icon="scale-balanced">
      A formal legal authorization allowing ChainPatrol to act for the organization in a broader or more formal capacity.
    </Card>
    
    **Key Differences from LOA:**
    - More formal legal standing
    - Broader scope of authority
    - Often notarized or witnessed
    - Recognized across more jurisdictions
    - Required for high-impact actions
    
    **When It's Required:**
    - Domain suspensions
    - TLD-level takedowns
    - Registrar-level actions
    - High-impact or escalated cases
    - International jurisdictions
    
    **What It Authorizes:**
    - Filing formal legal complaints
    - Representing the organization in takedown proceedings
    - Making binding statements on behalf of the organization
    - Escalating to legal authorities if needed
    
    <Warning>
    Some providers require a POA for escalated or high-impact takedowns such as domain suspensions.
    </Warning>
  </Tab>
</Tabs>

### Document Usage Across Platforms

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Website Takedowns" icon="globe">
    **Hosting providers and registrars**
    
    **Typically required:**
    - Trademark registration
    - LOA or POA
    - Business registration documents
    
    **Why:**
    - Verify legitimate brand ownership
    - Confirm authorization to request removal
    - Protect against false takedown requests
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="Social Media Takedowns" icon="share-nodes">
    **Platforms like Twitter/X, Facebook, Instagram**
    
    **Typically required:**
    - LOA (especially for Twitter/X)
    - Trademark registration (for verified impersonation)
    - Government-issued ID (for individual impersonation)
    
    **Why:**
    - Prevent abuse of reporting systems
    - Verify authorized representative
    - Confirm legitimate brand protection claim
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="App Store Takedowns" icon="mobile">
    **Google Play, Apple App Store**
    
    **Typically required:**
    - Trademark registration
    - LOA or POA
    - Proof of brand ownership
    
    **Why:**
    - Verify app is infringing on legitimate trademark
    - Confirm authorization to request removal
    - Protect against competitive abuse
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="TLD-Level Takedowns" icon="network-wired">
    **Top-level domain registries**
    
    **Typically required:**
    - POA (formal authorization)
    - Trademark registration
    - Detailed evidence of abuse
    
    **Why:**
    - High-impact action (entire domain suspension)
    - Legal liability considerations
    - Requires formal legal standing
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## What Legal Documents Are Used For

Legal documents are attached to takedown submissions so platforms can verify that:

<Steps>
  <Step title="Legitimate Representative">
    The requesting party truly represents the organization
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Verified Ownership">
    The brand or trademark is legitimately owned
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Authorized Action">
    ChainPatrol has the authority to request removal of the content
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Legal Compliance">
    The takedown request complies with the provider's legal requirements
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Warning>
Without these documents, many providers may refuse or delay action on a takedown request.
</Warning>

    - Role-based permissions
    - Multi-factor authentication required
    - Access logged and monitored
    - Regular access reviews
    
    <Check>
    Documents are used solely for the purpose of supporting takedown submissions.
    </Check>
  </Tab>
  
  <Tab title="Usage Policy" icon="file-shield">
    **Applied only when needed**
    
    Legal documents are **not directly part of the takedown object**â€”they are applied only during the submission process when a provider requires them.
    
    **How it works:**
    
    <Steps>
      <Step title="Takedown Created">
        Takedown request is created for a malicious asset
      </Step>

        System determines which provider to contact
      </Step>
      
      <Step title="Requirements Checked">
        System checks if provider requires legal documentation
      </Step>

## Document Management Best Practices

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Keep Documents Current" icon="arrows-rotate">
    **Regular updates are important**
    
    - Update POA/LOA documents annually
    - Refresh after organizational changes
    - Update contact information promptly
    - Renew before expiration dates
    
    <Tip>
    Expired or outdated documents can delay takedown processing.
    </Tip>
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="Maintain Multiple Formats" icon="file">
    **Different providers have different requirements**
    
    Keep documents in multiple formats:
    - PDF (most common)
    - Scanned originals
    - Notarized copies (for POA)
    - Translated versions (for international takedowns)
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="Coordinate with Legal Team" icon="users">
    **Ensure proper authorization**
    
    - Have legal team review documents
    - Ensure authorized signatories
    - Verify scope of authorization
    - Confirm compliance with company policies
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="Track Document Usage" icon="chart-line">
    **Monitor effectiveness**
    
    - Track which providers accept which documents
    - Note any rejection reasons
    - Update documents based on feedback
    - Maintain records of successful takedowns
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>
