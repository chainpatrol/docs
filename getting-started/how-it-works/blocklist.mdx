ChainPatrol's Blocklist is a real-time, community-powered database of confirmed phishing sites, scams, and malicious assets that protects users across the web3 ecosystem.

## What is the ChainPatrol Blocklist?

The ChainPatrol Blocklist is a continuously updated list of **350,000+ malicious websites**, social media accounts, and blockchain addresses that have been confirmed as threats through our expert review process. When an asset is added to the blocklist, it's automatically distributed to our network of security integrations to protect users in real-time.

## How Assets Get Blocklisted

Assets are added to the blocklist through a rigorous review process:

<Steps>
  <Step title="Detection">
    Potential threats are identified through automated detection, community reports, or partner submissions
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Analysis">
    Our AI-powered scanning engine analyzes assets using 50+ detection rules
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Review">
    A BLOCK proposal is created and reviewed by our security team
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Approval">
    After review approval, the asset status changes to BLOCKED
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Distribution">
    The blocked asset is automatically submitted to our integration partners
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Note>
Only assets that pass through this review and approval process are added to the blocklist.
</Note>

## False Positives

We take false positives seriously. If you believe an asset has been incorrectly blocklisted:

<Tabs>
  <Tab title="For Asset Owners" icon="user">
    If your legitimate website or asset has been blocklisted:
    
    <Steps>
      <Step title="Submit a Dispute">
        Visit [chainpatrol.com/dispute](https://chainpatrol.com/dispute) to submit a dispute
      </Step>
      
      <Step title="Provide Evidence">
        Include documentation proving your asset is legitimate:
        - Business registration
        - Domain age and history
        - Verified social media accounts
        - SSL certificates
        - Other legitimacy indicators
      </Step>
      
      <Step title="Review Process">
        Our team will review your dispute within 24 hours
      </Step>
      
      <Step title="Resolution">
        If approved, your asset will be removed from the blocklist and marked as ALLOWED
      </Step>
    </Steps>
  </Tab>
  
  <Tab title="For ChainPatrol Users" icon="users">
    If you've reported an asset that turns out to be legitimate:
    
    <Steps>
      <Step title="Navigate to Report">
        Find the asset's report in your dashboard
      </Step>
      
      <Step title="Submit ALLOW Proposal">
        Create an ALLOW proposal with your reasoning
      </Step>
      
      <Step title="Team Review">
        Our team will review and update the status if appropriate
      </Step>
    </Steps>
  </Tab>
</Tabs>

<Check>
When an asset is removed from the blocklist, the update is distributed to all integration partners within the same **15-30 minute timeframe**.
</Check>
